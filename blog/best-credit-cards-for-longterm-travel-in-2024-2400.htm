Best Credit Cards for Long-Term Travel in 2024

  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Blog Details

Traveling long-term in 2024 isn’t just about packing light or finding cheap flights—it’s about maximizing every dollar while navigating an ever-changing global landscape. With inflation, geopolitical tensions, and the rise of digital nomadism, choosing the right credit card can make or break your journey. Whether you’re a backpacker, remote worker, or retiree exploring the world, here’s a breakdown of the best credit cards to fuel your adventures.

Why Your Credit Card Choice Matters More Than Ever

The post-pandemic travel boom has reshaped the industry. Airlines and hotels are raising prices, while countries like Japan and Thailand are rolling out new visa programs to attract long-term visitors. Meanwhile, economic uncertainty means every swipe of your card should earn you something—whether it’s miles, cashback, or lounge access.

Key Trends Impacting Travel in 2024:

  • Digital Nomad Visas: Over 50 countries now offer remote work visas, from Portugal to Bali.
  • Inflation & Currency Fluctuations: Stretch your budget with cards that waive foreign transaction fees.
  • Sustainability: Eco-conscious travelers prefer cards that offset carbon emissions.
  • Geopolitical Shifts: Avoid cards tied to banks with sanctions in regions like Russia or the Middle East.

Top Credit Cards for Long-Term Travelers

1. Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card

Best for Flexible Rewards
- Annual Fee: $95
- Key Perks:
- 3x points on dining, streaming, and online groceries.
- 2x points on all travel purchases.
- 25% more value when redeeming points via Chase Ultimate Rewards®.
- Why It’s Great: Transfer points to 14+ airline/hotel partners, including United and Hyatt. Ideal for travelers hopping between continents.

2. Capital One Venture X Rewards Credit Card

Best for Luxury Travelers
- Annual Fee: $395 (but offsets with $300 annual travel credit)
- Key Perks:
- 10x miles on hotels/cars booked via Capital One Travel.
- Unlimited Priority Pass lounge access.
- Global Entry/TSA PreCheck credit.
- Why It’s Great: Combines premium benefits with straightforward rewards—perfect for those splitting time between Airbnbs and five-star resorts.

3. Wise (Formerly TransferWise) Debit Card

Best for No-Fee Spending Abroad
- Annual Fee: $0
- Key Perks:
- Free ATM withdrawals up to $250/month.
- Real exchange rates with no hidden fees.
- Why It’s Great: A must-have backup for countries where credit cards aren’t widely accepted (e.g., Vietnam or Brazil).

4. American Express® Gold Card

Best for Foodies
- Annual Fee: $250
- Key Perks:
- 4x points at restaurants worldwide.
- $120 annual dining credit (Grubhub, Cheesecake Factory, etc.).
- Why It’s Great: If you’re a digital nomad who lives for street food and Michelin-starred meals, this card pays for itself.

5. Bilt Mastercard®

Best for Renters
- Annual Fee: $0
- Key Perks:
- Earn points on rent payments (no transaction fees).
- 3x points on dining and 2x on travel.
- Why It’s Great: The only card that lets you turn rent into airline miles—game-changing for expats leasing apartments abroad.

Niche Picks for Specific Travel Styles

For Budget Backpackers: Discover it® Miles

  • No annual fee + 1.5x miles on every purchase.
  • First-year match on all miles earned.

For Eco-Travelers: Aspiration Plus

  • Offsets carbon emissions with every swipe.
  • 10% cashback on sustainable brands.

For Frequent Flyers: United Club℠ Infinite Card

  • Free checked bags, Premier Access, and United Club membership.

Red Flags to Avoid

Not all travel cards are created equal. Steer clear of:
- High foreign transaction fees (e.g., Credit One).
- Limited acceptance (e.g., Discover in Europe).
- Point devaluation traps (research airlines’ frequent-flyer changes).

Pro Tips to Maximize Your Card

  • Stack benefits: Pair a premium card (like Venture X) with a no-fee debit card (Wise).
  • Leverage transfer partners: Book business class for economy prices.
  • Time your applications: Some banks reject applicants with too many recent inquiries.

The right credit card is your secret weapon for long-term travel in 2024—turning everyday spending into unforgettable experiences. Choose wisely, and the world won’t just be your oyster; it’ll be your rewards playground.
